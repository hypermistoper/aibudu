{% raw %}Pandemic insurance is seeing a surge in demand, as Taiwan grapples with a new wave of COVID-19. As of April 11, more than NT$919 million in COVID policies had been sold nationwide. But with infection risk on the rise, many insurance firms are taking steps to mitigate the risk of losses. So far, three companies have decided to take their COVID policies off the market. <br /><br />Policy after policy comes out of the printer, keeping insurance company staff on their feet. With local case counts hitting new highs each day, demand is soaring for COVID insurance.<br /><br />Insurance company employee<br />Over here I have about 30 to 40 forms. I’m going to take a break and process them in the morning.<br /><br />Insurance company employee<br />We handle the cases once they reach a set quantity. We can’t handle them one at a time as they come. That would be a tremendous amount of work for insurance workers.<br /><br />Government data shows that as of April 11, more than 1.19 million COVID insurance policies had been sold for a total of NT$919 million. So far, these policies have paid out NT$559 million in compensation. With infection risk on the rise, some insurance firms are discontinuing their COVID policies.<br /><br />Starting May 1, Hotai Insurance will stop selling its NT$614 COVID insurance, which offers NT$90,000 to confirmed cases ordered to isolate. This Friday, CTBC Insurance will discontinue its NT$606 policy, which offers NT$80,000 to cases in isolation. MSIG Mingtai Insurance already ended its NT$517 policy on Wednesday.<br /><br />Cheng Jui-shen<br />Insurance company<br />One company stopped selling it, and then another, setting off a cascade of discontinuations. If policies are sold again after this wave of suspensions, those policies might offer NT$10,000 for isolation. The compensation for a COVID diagnosis might be between NT$30,000 and NT$50,000.<br /><br />Member of public<br />If the payouts are smaller, of course people are not going to be as interested.<br /><br />Member of public<br />I have medical insurance. I don’t usually buy these kinds of packages. I don’t need them.<br /><br />Not a few people say they already have private medical insurance. If insurance firms decide to lower their pandemic payouts, demand for COVID insurance is expected to cool.{% endraw %}
