{% raw %}China will offer an additional 10 million yuan (about 1.57 million U.S. dollars) of humanitarian assistance to Ukraine, Foreign Ministry spokesman Wang Wenbin said at a press briefing Monday.<br />Wang made the remarks when answering a question on China's plans for offering more assistance to Ukraine amid the conflict.<br />Wang said, &quot;China is greatly concerned about the suffering of ordinary residents amid the conflict between Russia and Ukraine. Dealing with the possible large-scale humanitarian crisis is the top priority for the international community at present. China has initiated six proposals to ease the humanitarian situation in Ukraine, and has taken real actions. China has provided a batch of humanitarian assistance to Ukraine. China decided to provide additional humanitarian material assistance worth 10 million yuan (about 1.57 million U.S. dollars) to Ukraine. This is China's additional humanitarian assistance to Ukraine in the light of the development of the situation and actual needs. China will continue to play a constructive role in easing the situation in Ukraine, and has been ready to make our own efforts to overcome the humanitarian crisis.&quot; <br /><br
